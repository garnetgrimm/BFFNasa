NASA Will Never Accomplish Anything

And It's not even their fault.
After succcessfully landing a man on the moon in 1969 using less computational power than an IPhone, NASA became less important to the politicians determining its budget. It is for this reason NASA’s budget has declined steadily from 4% to 0.4% today. To make matters worse, the government does not only decide how much NASA receives for funding, but also exactly how much funding is to be applied to which missions. This system worked marvelously in a time where regardless of your political leanings, going to the moon was NASA’s primary mission. However, in today's political world this is not necessarily the case. When Barack Obama became President of the United States, of America his administration effectively told NASA to drop all current progress and to focus more heavily on becoming a powerful influence on forefront global climate change, commercial spaceflight, mars travel, and the launching of new telescopes. Obama also put a heavy influence on the idea that putting weapons in space can only lead to mass destruction. When Donald Trump took office in 2016, his administration again changed practically everything being worked on by NASA. President Trump essentially believes NASA should be almost entirely a military force with “cutting-edge warfighting capabilities” to put an emphasis on “peace through strength”. From an objective standpoint, neither of these philosophies are wrong, but what is unjust is the constant and extreme switching of political ideals for an agency aimed primarily at scientific research. Here is how you can stop it.

Surprising Facts & Statistics

NASA is Good For More Than Just Rockets

NASA actually invented many devices used every day by the modern world such as CAT scans, water purification systems, wireless headsets, laptops, dust busters and yes, camera phones

The US Has Too Many Nukes

The United States today is in possession of 45% of the worlds nuclear weapons (6800 total). Maintenance costs for these warheads would enough to build the space station three times over.

Nasa's Budget is Shrinking Exponentially

NASA went to the moon in 1969 with just 4% of Americas federal budget, however today that number has shrunk to just 0.4% today

NASA is a Safe Bet

Because NASA is funded by the government, safety procedures are followed precisely. If a private company saw an opportunity to cut a corner in the name of business, they could.

Endless Projects Have Been Cancelled

NASA has wasted appoximately $20 billion dollars in projects cancelled by the US government. More information here

Most of the Federal Budget Goes to the Military

The military currently occupys 54% of the federal budget. Necessities such as education and unemployment costs receive less than 5%
